Volatility Watch – Sunday, September 14, 2025

Equity markets staged a whipsaw week as hotter CPI data collided with cooler retail sales. The VIX spiked above 19 midweek before sliding back to 16 as dip buyers stepped in across mega-cap tech and financials.

Sector leadership rotated: healthcare outperformed with a 2.3% gain, powered by biotech merger chatter, while consumer discretionary slipped on softer guidance from big-box retailers. Energy moderated after the Department of Energy announced a modest SPR release to temper gasoline prices.

Across the globe, the ECB delivered a “dovish hike,” raising rates 25 bps but signaling a potential pause. The euro strengthened, pushing EUR/USD back above 1.10. Meanwhile, the yen weakened despite verbal intervention from Japanese officials, keeping USD/JPY near 151.

Keep an eye on this Thursday’s Philly Fed index and Friday’s quadruple witching options expiration—both could amplify short-term swings as traders reposition into quarter-end.
